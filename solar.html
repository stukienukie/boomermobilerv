What We Install

Complete Solar Systems

Every component, properly integrated. Boomer handles the full system — not just slapping panels on the roof.

Solar Panels

Rigid & flexible, roof-mounted

High-efficiency monocrystalline panels sized to your energy needs and roof space. Rigid aluminum-framed for most applications; flexible panels available for curved roofs or weight-sensitive setups.

  • 100W to 800W+ system capacities
  • Watertight roof penetrations and sealing
  • UV-resistant wiring and connectors

Lithium Battery Banks

LiFePO4 storage systems

Lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries are the gold standard for RV solar storage — 3-5x the usable capacity of AGM, 2,000+ charge cycles, and half the weight.

  • 100Ah to 400Ah+ configurations
  • Built-in battery management system (BMS)
  • Properly fused and protected installation

Inverters & Converters

DC to AC pure sine wave

Pure sine wave inverters convert your battery bank to clean 120V AC power — safe for sensitive electronics like laptops, CPAP machines, and modern appliances. Properly sized for your load requirements.

  • 1000W to 3000W inverter options
  • Inverter/charger combos available
  • Integrated with shore power input

Charge Controllers

MPPT solar regulation

MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking) charge controllers extract maximum efficiency from your solar panels and protect your batteries from overcharge — critical for long-term battery health.

  • MPPT efficiency up to 98%
  • Battery type programming (Li, AGM, flooded)
  • Bluetooth monitoring integration

FAQ

Common Questions

Can solar run my air conditioning?

Yes, but it requires a large system — typically 600W+ of panels and 300Ah+ of lithium batteries paired with a 2000–3000W inverter. We'll assess your specific AC unit's draw and design accordingly. It's very doable with a properly sized system.

How long does installation take?

Most starter and mid-range systems take 1–2 days. Larger off-grid builds with full roof coverage and major battery banks may take 2–3 days. We'll give you a realistic timeline during the consultation.

Do I still need my generator?

With a well-designed solar system, you'll rarely need your generator. Many customers report running it only for heavy loads during extended overcast stretches, or not at all. It becomes a backup rather than primary power.

What if my roof isn't flat or has vents in the way?

We work with what you have. Many RVs have crowded roofs — we design panel layouts around existing vents, AC units, and antennas to maximize power while avoiding obstacles. Flexible panels are an option for curved roofs.

Will solar work in Utah winters?

Absolutely. Utah gets excellent winter sun even in colder months. Cold temperatures actually improve panel efficiency. The main consideration is reduced daylight hours — we size battery banks accordingly for year-round use.
